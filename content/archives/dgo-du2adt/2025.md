## ÉPREUVE DE FRANÇAIS

### Texte

> L'Afrique de l'Ouest bénéficie d'un ensoleillement parmi les plus élevés au monde. Cette ressource naturelle constitue un atout majeur pour répondre aux défis énergétiques de la région, où de nombreux foyers et villages restent non connectés au réseau électrique national. Face à cette situation, l'énergie solaire apparaît comme une alternative prometteuse, à la fois durable, accessible et adaptée aux réalités locales.
>
> Au Sénégal, par exemple, plusieurs projets d'électrification rurale ont été lancés ces dernières années, grâce à l'installation de panneaux solaires dans les zones éloignées. Ces installations permettent d'alimenter des équipements essentiels : lampes, réfrigérateurs pour conserver les vaccins, ou encore pompes à eau. Dans les écoles, l'arrivée de l'électricité améliore considérablement les conditions d'apprentissage, notamment avec l'introduction de matériel informatique et l'éclairage des salles de classe en soirée.
>
> L'un des avantages clés de l'énergie solaire est sa modularité. Contrairement aux grandes centrales thermiques ou hydrauliques, les systèmes solaires peuvent être déployés à petite échelle, maison par maison, ou à l'échelle d'un village entier. Cette souplesse technique réduit les coûts d'infrastructures lourdes et accélère la mise en service. En outre, le prix des panneaux photovoltaïques a fortement baissé au cours de la dernière décennie, rendant la technologie plus abordable.
>
> Cependant, plusieurs défis freinent encore une adoption massive. Le stockage de l'énergie constitue un enjeu central. Les batteries, souvent nécessaires pour garantir une alimentation continue, restent coûteuses et doivent être remplacées régulièrement. De plus, le manque de techniciens qualifiés dans certaines zones rend l'entretien des installations difficile, voire impossible sans aide extérieure. Former des jeunes aux métiers du solaire devient donc une priorité.
>
> Des initiatives régionales et internationales soutiennent cette transition. L'Agence Sénégalaise d'Electrification Rurale (ASER), appuyée par des partenaires comme la Banque mondiale ou l'Union européenne, multiplie les projets dans les régions de Kédougou, Tambacounda ou Matam. De plus, des start-ups africaines innovent dans le domaine, proposant des kits solaires « plug and play », faciles à installer et à utiliser, même pour des personnes non spécialisées.
>
> Au-delà de l'accès à l'électricité, le développement du solaire ouvre la voie à des opportunités économiques : création d'emplois locaux, amélioration de l'agriculture grâce à l'irrigation solaire, et réduction de la dépendance aux énergies fossiles importées. À terme, si les efforts de formation, de financement et de maintenance sont poursuivis, l'énergie solaire pourrait jouer un rôle décisif dans le développement durable de l'Afrique de l'Ouest.
>
> _Texte inspiré d'articles de l'IRENA, de Le Monde Afrique, et de l'ASER Sénégal._

## ÉPREUVE D'ANGLAIS

### Texte

> Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems. It is thus the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks commonly associated with intelligent beings. The term is frequently applied to the project of developing systems endowed with the intellectual processes characteristic of humans, such as the ability to reason, discover meaning, generalize, or learn from past experience. AI research attempts to reach one of these three goals: Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), applied AI, or cognitive simulation. AGI aims to build machines that think. The ultimate ambition of AGI is to produce a machine whose overall intellectual ability is indistinguishable from that of a human being's.
>
> Since their development in the 1940s, digital computers have been programmed to carry out very complex tasks such as discovering proofs for mathematical theorems or even playing chess with great proficiency. Presently, some programs have attained the performance levels of human experts and professionals in executing certain specific tasks, so that artificial intelligence in this limited sense is found in applications as diverse as medical diagnosis, computer search engines, voice or handwriting recognition, and chatbots. Furthermore, many diverse problems have been solved by artificial intelligence programs.
>
> Psychologists generally characterize human intelligence not by just one trait but by the combination of many diverse abilities. Research in AI has focused chiefly on the following components of intelligence: learning, reasoning, problem solving, perception, and using language. Large Language Models (LLM) like ChatGPT can respond fluently in a human language to questions and statements. Released three years ago, ChatGPT almost immediately disturbed academics, journalists, and others because of concern that it was impossible to distinguish human writing from ChatGPT-generated writing. Although such models do not actually understand language as humans do but merely select words that are more probable than others, they have reached the point where their command of a language is indistinguishable from that of a normal human. What, then, is involved in genuine understanding, if even a computer that uses language like a native human speaker is not acknowledged to understand? There is no universally agreed answer to this difficult question.
>
> AI poses certain risks in terms of ethical and socioeconomic consequences. For instance, deepfakes often transform existing source content where one person is swapped for another. They also create entirely original content where someone is represented doing or saying something they didn't do or say. Deepfakes are AI-generated media produced using two different deep-learning algorithms: one that creates the best possible replica of a real image or video and another that detects whether the replica is fake and, if it is, reports on the differences between it and the original. One of the most troublesome drawbacks of deepfakes is blackmail and reputation damage. Examples of this are when a target image is put in an illegal, inappropriate or otherwise compromising situation such as lying to the public, engaging in explicit sexual acts or taking drugs. These videos are used to extort a victim, ruin a person's reputation, get revenge or simply cyberbullying.
>
> Another issue with ChatGPT is "hallucinations": rather than communicating to a user that it does not know something, the model responds with probable but inaccurate text based on the user's prompts. This issue may be partially attributed to using Large Language Models as search engines rather than in their intended role of text generation. Despite continuing advances in computer processing speed and memory capacity, there are as yet no programs that can match full human flexibility over wider domains or in tasks requiring much everyday knowledge.
>
> There has been considerable success in programming computers to draw inferences. However, true reasoning involves more than just drawing inferences: it involves drawing inferences relevant to the solution of the particular problem. This is one of the hardest problems confronting AI.
